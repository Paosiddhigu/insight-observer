## Recommendations

- **Lead promotional messaging with character and emotional stakes.** The viewer’s strongest spontaneous comment was “**i love the character**,” suggesting character-centered creative should be prominent in future spots and social assets.
- **Preserve the atmospheric tone.** The “**overall atmosphere of the scene**” was a clear point of connection. Marketing should continue emphasizing the film’s mythic scale, tension, danger, and emotional isolation rather than relying only on action spectacle.
- **Develop the character hook more explicitly.** Additional materials could clarify who the character is, what they want, and what makes their return home difficult, while avoiding excessive plot explanation.
- **Use the promise/homecoming dialogue selectively.** “**Promise me you'll come back**” and “**What if I can't?**” appear well suited to reinforcing emotional stakes and suspense. These lines could anchor shorter social edits or character-focused teasers.
- **Pair atmosphere with a clearer narrative proposition.** Since the viewer did not independently mention the epic scope, ensemble cast, IMAX presentation, or release date, follow-up marketing should connect the mood to a more concrete reason to see the film in theaters.
- **Test character-specific assets.** Short clips or featurettes focused on the central performance, relationships, and the compelling scene around **0:48–1:24** may convert this type of atmospheric interest into stronger intent to watch.
